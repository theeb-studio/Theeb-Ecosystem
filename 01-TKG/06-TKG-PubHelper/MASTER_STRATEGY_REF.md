## 1. STRATEGIC MISSION

PubHelper is the **evidence production engine** of the Theeb Ecosystem — the entity that closes the gap between clinical practice (THDG) and scientific knowledge (TKG). In the 2026 Saudi healthcare logic:

1. **Research Democratization:** Saudi clinicians generate thousands of clinical questions daily. Most die as corridor conversations. PubHelper captures them, structures them (PICO), assigns the correct study design (evidence pyramid), and manages the full lifecycle to publication.
2. **National Evidence Factory:** Saudi Arabia imports virtually all clinical knowledge from Western evidence bases. PubHelper systematically converts Saudi clinical encounters into Saudi-specific evidence — addressing the sovereignty gap at scale.
3. **Flywheel Accelerator:** PubHelper is the missing piece that connects THDG (clinical questions) → TKG (scientific production) → Evidence Platform (digital distribution) → CII (clinical intelligence). Without it, the flywheel depends on manual research initiation. With it, the flywheel is automated.

**Chess Player Role:** PubHelper occupies the research production square — the piece that turns the raw material of clinical practice (questions, observations, data) into the refined product of scientific knowledge (publications, guidelines, evidence summaries). It is the conveyor belt that makes the Knowledge Group's output systematic rather than opportunistic.

## 4. THE SOVEREIGN EVIDENCE GRADING RUBRIC

This is PubHelper's **quality gate**. Every piece of research must be graded before it enters the Shared Intelligence Layer. The rubric ensures that output meets international indexing standards and can move scientific societies from Category C to Category A (SCFHS).

### The Evidence Pyramid (PubHelper Assessment Framework)

```
         ▲ Clinical Practice Guidelines    ← Grade A: National policy impact
        ╱ ╲
       ╱   ╲  Meta-Analysis / Systematic   ← Level 5: Apex / Systematic
      ╱     ╲  Review of RCTs
     ╱       ╲
    ╱ Randomized ╲                          ← Level 4: Interventional
   ╱  Controlled   ╲
  ╱    Trials        ╲
 ╱ Cohort / Case-Control╲                  ← Level 3: Observational / Strong
╱  Studies                ╲
╱ Case Reports / Case Series ╲             ← Level 2: Descriptive
╱  Narrative Reviews, Opinions  ╲
╱ Animal / Laboratory Studies     ╲        ← Level 1: Emerging
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### Grading Matrix (5 Dimensions × 5 Levels)

| Dimension | Level 1: Emerging | Level 3: Observational / Strong | Level 5: Apex / Systematic |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Study Design** | Case reports, expert opinions, editorials | Prospective cohort or case-control with defined variables | Meta-analysis or systematic review of RCTs |
| **Methodology Rigor** | Descriptive only; no control group or statistical significance | PICO format; risk-adjustment; p < 0.05 | Double-blinded; multi-center; low risk of bias; heterogeneity controlled |
| **Data Integrity** | Manual data entry; limited validation; small sample | eCRF-captured; >85% completeness; ICD-10/11 verified | Federated data from multiple THDG/external sites; independent audit |
| **Local Relevance** | General medical topic, no Saudi population data | Study on Saudi-specific patient cohorts (e.g., Welunion patients) | Addresses high-priority Saudi disease (diabetes, obesity) with national policy impact |
| **Publication Status** | Internal white paper or non-indexed journal | Scopus/PubMed indexed journal with peer review | Integrated into National Clinical Guideline published by Waraqa/CHI |

### Strength of Recommendation (SOR) Scoring

| Grade | Score (0-25) | Meaning | Use Case |
|-------|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Grade A** | 20-25 | Strong recommendation | Suitable for national clinical guidelines |
| **Grade B** | 15-19 | Moderate recommendation | Suitable for institutional protocols |
| **Grade C** | <15 | Emerging evidence | Requires further primary study |
