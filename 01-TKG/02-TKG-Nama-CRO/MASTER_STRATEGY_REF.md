### How the Four Lines Reinforce Each Other

```
CTU Development builds a trial-ready hospital
    → SMO embeds trained coordinators inside that hospital
    → CRO brings sponsored trials to the now-ready site
    → RWE extracts real-world evidence from clinical data
    → Evidence feeds Evidence Platform + PubHelper + Waraqa
    → Publications attract more sponsors
    → More sponsors need more sites → more CTU builds
    → [FLYWHEEL: every engagement creates the next one]
```

**This is why Nama Medical is irreplicable:** A pure CRO can only run trials at existing sites. Nama Medical builds the sites (CTU), staffs them (SMO), runs trials on them (CRO), and extracts population-level intelligence (RWE). Each capability makes the others more valuable.

## 3. THE FOUR SERVICE LINES IN DETAIL

### 3A. CRO — Interventional Clinical Trials (Phase I-IV)

End-to-end operational support for interventional studies across KSA, UAE, and Egypt.

| Capability | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Study startup & regulatory submissions | SFDA, IRB, ethics committee navigation |
| Site selection, qualification, contracting | Using CTU-built network |
| Project management & monitoring | On-ground teams for predictable timelines |
| Data management & safety oversight | ICH-GCP compliant, CDISC standards |
| Patient recruitment & retention | Leveraging Welunion clinic network |
| Medical writing & publication | Via Waraqa inter-company SLA |

**Differentiation:** On-ground teams ensure predictable timelines, consistent site performance, and global-quality execution at regional cost.

### 3B. SMO — Site Management Organization

Embeds trained clinical research coordinators directly inside hospitals.

| Capability | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Embedded CRCs and study coordinators | Dedicated staff inside hospital sites |
| Site readiness assessments & gap closure | Ensuring sites meet sponsor requirements |
| Enrollment management | Patient screening, recruitment, retention |
| KPI tracking | Activation time, recruitment rate, data quality |
| Quality compliance | Continuous monitoring and audit support |

**Differentiation:** SMO is the bridge between CTU infrastructure and CRO trial execution — without embedded coordinators, even well-built CTUs underperform.

### 3C. CTU — Clinical Trial Unit Development

Builds sustainable research capacity inside hospitals, enabling them to independently host trials.

| Component | What We Build |
|-----------|--------------|
| **CTU Development** | Fully operational trial units: staffing, process mapping, workflow design, SOPs, quality systems, audit readiness |
| **Ethics Committee / IRB** | Setup, governance, membership, SOP creation, training, ethical review processes, submission workflows |
| **Training Programs** | GCP & research ethics, investigator development, CRC certification, study startup, RWE capability |
| **Research Publications & Journals** | Via Waraqa — indexed journal services for hospital research output |

**Key message:** *"We don't just run trials — we enable hospitals to become trial-ready."*

**Market context:** Fewer than 25 of 300+ Saudi hospitals are consistently trial-ready. CTU development is a SAR 50-100M+ opportunity as every hospital network wants research capability for accreditation, reputation, and revenue.

### 3D. RWE — Real-World Evidence & Observational Research

Generates high-quality real-world data for market access, value dossiers, payer negotiations, and post-marketing requirements.

| Capability | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Registries and long-term cohorts | Disease-specific Saudi population registries |
| Retrospective & prospective observational studies | Claims data, EMR data, patient surveys |
| Health Economics & Outcomes Research (HEOR) | Cost-effectiveness, budget impact, utility studies |
| Data analytics & reporting | Regulatory and payer-grade evidence packages |

**Differentiation:** Access to diverse Saudi patient populations + expertise in regulatory-acceptable data frameworks + partnership with local CTUs and medical cities.

**Market context:** Only 18% of Saudi studies generate usable RWE due to fragmented data systems. Nama Medical's integration with CII and Evidence Platform resolves this — RWE flows directly into the ecosystem's intelligence layer.

## 8. THE CTU-TO-ECOSYSTEM PIPELINE

### When Nama Medical Builds a CTU, the Entire Ecosystem Benefits

```
NAMA BUILDS CTU AT HOSPITAL X
    │
    ├──► Hospital becomes trial-ready (CRO pipeline)
    ├──► SMO coordinators embedded (permanent presence)
    ├──► RWE data flows from hospital's clinical operations
    │
    ├──► Nama Academy trains hospital's research staff (GCP, CRC cert)
    ├──► Evidence Platform provides clinical decision support to PIs
    ├──► Waraqa publishes hospital's research output (indexed journals)
    ├──► PubHelper converts clinical questions into research projects
    │
    ├──► Integra RCM provides billing/coding for research operations
    ├──► VBH Co uses hospital outcomes for VBC evidence
    ├──► CII ingests clinical data → population intelligence
    │
    └──► Hospital is now embedded in the Theeb Ecosystem
         Switching cost: MASSIVE (infrastructure, training, data, relationships)
         Lifetime value: 10+ years of research, education, and clinical intelligence
```

**Key insight:** Every CTU build is not a one-time project — it's an **ecosystem installation**. The hospital doesn't just get a trial unit; it gets connected to the entire Theeb infrastructure. This is why competitors who only build CTUs can't compete — they don't have the downstream value creation.
