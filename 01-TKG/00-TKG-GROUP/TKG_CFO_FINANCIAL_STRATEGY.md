## ENTITY-BY-ENTITY CFO ANALYSIS

### 1. WARAQA (TKG-01) — The Profit Engine

| Metric | 2026 | 2027 | 2028 | 2029 | 2030 |
|--------|------|------|------|------|------|
| Revenue | 1,918 | 5,640 | 15,950 | 25,600 | 36,350 |
| EBITDA | 318 | 2,990 | 10,750 | 17,900 | 26,150 |
| EBITDA Margin | 16.6% | 53.0% | 67.4% | 69.9% | 71.9% |

**CFO Assessment:**
- **Strongest margin profile** in TKG — 71.9% EBITDA by Year 5
- **AI licensing (Stream 7)** is the margin accelerator: SAR 7M in Y5 at near-100% margin
- **Self-sustaining** from Year 1 — no external capital required
- **Funds Nama Academy** through SAR 400-500K/year internal allocation
- **Risk:** Society conversion rate is the key variable. If only 5 societies by Y3 (vs. 10 target), revenue drops ~30%

### 2. NAMA CRO (TKG-02) — The Scale Engine

| Metric | 2026 | 2027 | 2028 | 2029 | 2030 |
|--------|------|------|------|------|------|
| Revenue | 3,625 | 9,650 | 18,600 | 30,000 | 43,650 |
| EBITDA | (75) | 1,650 | 5,300 | 10,500 | 17,450 |
| EBITDA Margin | -2.1% | 17.1% | 28.5% | 35.0% | 40.0% |

**CFO Assessment:**
- **Largest revenue entity** by Year 5 (SAR 43.7M)
- **Investment payback:** SAR 18.75M invested → SAR 17.5M EBITDA in Y5 alone = <2 year payback at scale
- **Alfaisaliah de-risks Year 1:** guaranteed trial pipeline reduces revenue risk in critical early phase
- **30-month milestone agreement** ensures capital discipline (tranche-based release)
- **Risk:** Interventional trial revenue is lumpy — quarterly variance expected. RWE provides smoother baseline

### 3. NAMA ACADEMY (TKG-03) — The Distribution Engine

| Metric | 2026 | 2027 | 2028 | 2029 | 2030 |
|--------|------|------|------|------|------|
| Revenue | 1,700 | 4,700 | 10,300 | 17,200 | 26,000 |
| EBITDA | (50) | 1,200 | 3,800 | 7,200 | 11,500 |
| EBITDA Margin | -2.9% | 25.5% | 36.9% | 41.9% | 44.2% |

**CFO Assessment:**
- **AaaS is the margin lever:** SAR 0 in Y1 → SAR 5M in Y5 at ~60% margin (recurring institutional contracts)
- **MGA bundle creates recurring per-physician revenue** not captured in traditional training models
- **Funded by Waraqa** — no external capital, no dilution
- **Strategic value exceeds financial value:** 5,000 physicians/year by Y5 = the most valuable distribution asset in Saudi healthcare. This is worth more as an ecosystem enabler than as standalone revenue
- **Risk:** SHA accreditation timeline affects life support revenue. AaaS requires 12-18 month sales cycles

### 4. NAMA BIO (TKG-04) — The Regulatory Asset

| Metric | 2026 | 2027 | 2028 | 2029 | 2030 |
|--------|------|------|------|------|------|
| Revenue | 2,700 | 4,500 | 8,100 | 10,238 | 13,650 |
| EBITDA | (1,300) | (2,362) | (3,705) | (3,904) | (3,736) |
| EBITDA Margin | -48.1% | -52.5% | -45.7% | -38.1% | -27.4% |

**CFO Assessment — HONEST CONCERN:**
- **Nama Bio is structurally EBITDA-negative through Year 5** in the conservative model. COGS + G&A exceeds revenue because the cost base (clinical ward operations, bioanalytical outsourcing, direct labor) is high relative to study revenue.
- **However:** The detailed financial model provided shows positive EBITDA from Year 2 onward. The discrepancy is due to different cost assumptions. **This requires reconciliation.**
- **The strategic value is real:** SFDA accreditation, Saudi population PK data, and Vision 2030 drug localization mandate make this entity strategically essential even if financially challenging in early years
- **Recommendation:** Reconcile detailed financial model with conservative assumptions. If breakeven requires 25+ studies/year, validate CARE Medical ward capacity to support this volume. Consider whether cost structure can be leaned further.
- **Risk:** CARE Medical and KSU partnerships are unsigned. If either fails, the asset-light model collapses

### 5. SAUDI EVIDENCE (TKG-05) — The Platform Play

**CFO Assessment:**
- **Classic platform economics:** Invest heavily in Y1-Y2 (SAR 5.4M cumulative loss), break even in Y3, then margins expand rapidly as user base grows with near-zero marginal cost
- **75,000 users by Y5** — if achieved, platform becomes the most valuable single asset in TKG (comparable to UpToDate's model)

- **Risk:** DeepEvidence partnership terms. If API costs are higher than modeled, COGS increases. User adoption curve is unproven

## RECOMMENDATION TO THE BOARD

1. **Proceed with Alfaisaliah deal** — the Nama CRO investment de-risks Y1 revenue and accelerates the entire ecosystem
2. **Prioritize government grant applications** for Saudi Evidence and Nama Bio — SAR 12M in non-dilutive funding covers the J-curve investment phase

6. **Do NOT take external equity in any entity other than Nama CRO** — the zero-dilution strategy preserves maximum founder value in the highest-multiple assets
