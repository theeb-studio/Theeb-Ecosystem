## 1. IDENTITY

**Waraqa Publishing House** is the scientific publishing and knowledge infrastructure company of TKG.

Waraqa is NOT a journal company. Waraqa is NOT a preprint platform. Waraqa is the **publishing engine** — the entity that converts raw research and clinical knowledge into validated, credentialed, commercially viable intellectual assets.

### What Waraqa Does

```
RAW KNOWLEDGE IN                    PUBLISHED ASSETS OUT
─────────────────                    ────────────────────
Research from Nama CRO          →   Peer-reviewed journal articles
Clinical guidelines from TTG-CII →   Published national guidelines
Society conference proceedings  →   Indexed, citable publications
Physician-generated evidence    →   Preprint + post-pub review
Regulatory frameworks           →   Compliance publications
```

### What Waraqa Is Not

| Confused With | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "Waraqa Platform" | That is **TKG-05 Evidence Platform** — a separate, increasingly independent entity |

## 2. THE PROBLEM WARAQA EXISTS TO SOLVE

### Saudi Arabia's Research Credibility Crisis

Saudi Arabia has the **highest rate of article retractions** among large research-producing nations (Nature, 2023). The scale:

- **3,748 Saudi-affiliated papers** impacted by Hindawi and MDPI delistings from Clarivate
- Journals removed from Scopus and Web of Science — destroying years of researcher effort
- No nationally controlled quality assurance infrastructure for research publishing
- Over-reliance on foreign mega-publishers with misaligned incentives (volume over quality)

### The Structural Gap

| What Saudi Researchers Need | What Currently Exists |
|----------------------------|----------------------|
| Sovereign publishing infrastructure | Dependency on Elsevier, Springer, MDPI, Hindawi |
| National preprint server | None — Chinese (ChinaXiv), Japanese (JXiv), no Saudi equivalent |
| Peer-reviewed society journals at international standard | ~30 Saudi journals; most not indexed in Tier 1 databases |
| Research integrity infrastructure | No post-publication review system |
| Arabic-language clinical publishing | Almost none at international standard |

### Waraqa's Position

Waraqa's founder published the **Nature correspondence** (Alkahtani, 2024) calling for national action on this crisis. Waraqa is the only Saudi publishing house built specifically to address this gap — with clinical research expertise, not general academic publishing.

## 3. PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE

Waraqa operates four product lines. Each can activate independently. Each can combine with TKG entities for larger engagements.

### Product 1: Society Journal Infrastructure

**What:** White-label journal publishing for Saudi scientific societies
**How it works:**
- Society provides the brand, editorial board, and manuscript flow
- Waraqa provides: editorial management, peer review coordination, production (typesetting, XML, DOI), hosting, indexing strategy
- Revenue: subscription model (SAR/year) + APC pass-through

**Technology:** Nvcleus/Kotahi platform (PubSweet framework) — web-based editorial workflow, multi-format output (HTML, PDF, ePub, XML)

**Target:** 50+ Saudi scientific societies — most lack publishing infrastructure

### Product 2: National Preprint Server

**What:** Saudi Arabia's first nationally recognized preprint platform
**How it works:**
- Researchers deposit manuscripts before formal peer review
- Platform provides: DOI, ORCID integration, plagiarism screening (iThenticate), AI rigor checks (SciScore), open peer review
- Institutional anchors: CUA, KAU (proposals submitted Dec 2025)

**Strategic Value:** Preprints → rapid knowledge sharing → builds the pipeline that feeds formal journals

### Product 3: Clinical Guidelines Publishing

**What:** Publication and distribution of Saudi clinical practice guidelines
**How it works:**
- Source: guidelines generated through Nama CRO research, TTG-CII evidence synthesis, or society expert panels
- Waraqa publishes, indexes, and versions the guidelines
- Evidence Platform (TKG-05) distributes them digitally
- Nama Academy (TKG-03) teaches them as CME content

**Strategic Value:** This is where TKG's flywheel begins — CRO generates → Waraqa publishes → Academy teaches → Platform distributes → CRO measures outcomes → cycle continues

### Product 4: Research Integrity Services

**What:** Post-publication peer review, data quality auditing, retraction monitoring
**How it works:**
- Institutional clients (universities, medical cities) subscribe
- Waraqa audits their research output for integrity risks
- Provides training (with Nama Academy) on research ethics and methodology

**Strategic Value:** Directly addresses the retractions crisis — turns a national problem into a revenue stream

## 5. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### Why Waraqa Wins Against Global Publishers

| Competitor | Their Model | Waraqa's Advantage |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Elsevier / Springer | Subscription + APC — extractive pricing, no local control | Sovereign infrastructure — data stays in KSA, pricing serves Saudi interests |
| MDPI / Hindawi | Volume-driven OA — led to retractions crisis | Quality-first, society-anchored, integrity as core product |
| UpToDate / Cochrane | Synthesize global evidence for global audience | Saudi-specific clinical guidelines, Arabic-first, locally validated |
| F1000 / ScienceOpen | Technology providers (proposed as Waraqa partners) | Waraqa is the operator, not the vendor — owns the relationship |

### Waraqa's Moat

1. **Founder credibility** — Nature-published voice on Saudi research integrity
2. **Society relationships** — Saudi Heart Association, university partnerships, MOE/MOH proposals
3. **Ecosystem integration** — no standalone publisher can offer CRO + CME + insurance bundle
4. **Regulatory positioning** — SCFHS, CCHI, MOH alignment through TKG entities
5. **Vision 2030 alignment** — sovereign scientific infrastructure narrative
6. **AI content IP ownership** — Waraqa owns the rights to license its content for AI (see Section 5B)

### Local Market Players — Not Competitors

Saudi companies operating in the research content space are **resellers/distributors**, not publishers:

| Company | What They Do | Relationship to Waraqa |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|
| **iDev (التنمية المعلوماتية)** | Distributes Clarivate, Elsevier, Wiley, EBSCO subscriptions to institutions. Also sells iThenticate, Turnitin. Won SAR 36M+ in SDL tenders. | **Potential channel partner** — could distribute Waraqa journals to their 40+ institutional clients |
| **Naseej (نسيج للتقنية)** | Listed company. Builds library management systems (Medad platform), digitization, campus IT. Won SAR 33M+ in SDL tenders. | **Potential technology partner** — Waraqa journals integrated into Medad library systems |
| **Dar Al-Rushd (دار الرشد)** | Traditional bookstore + publisher (since 1979). 9 branches. Won SDL tender portions as database reseller. | **Not relevant** — different market entirely (general books, not scientific publishing) |

> **Key insight:** None of these companies produce original scientific content. They all resell foreign publishers' databases. Waraqa is the only Saudi entity building sovereign scientific publishing infrastructure.

> **Market intelligence note:** Many hospitals and government entities buy database subscriptions directly from publishers rather than through the Saudi Digital Library (SDL), indicating SDL pricing may be inflated. This creates a fragmented market where Waraqa can approach institutions directly with bundled offerings (content + AI discovery) at competitive pricing.

## 5B. AI CONTENT LICENSING — THE NEW REVENUE FRONTIER

### The Global Shift

The scientific publishing industry is undergoing its biggest transformation since open access. Major publishers now sell **AI licensing rights** as a separate, high-margin revenue stream:

| Publisher | AI Licensing Revenue | Key AI Partners |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| Wiley | $44M (2024), $16M in Q1 2026 alone (+1,500% YoY) | Anthropic, AWS, Perplexity, OpenEvidence |
| NEJM Group | Multi-year content deal (1990-present) | OpenEvidence |
| JAMA Network | Multi-year deal (JAMA + 11 specialty journals) | OpenEvidence |

### The OpenEvidence Model — and Why It Matters

OpenEvidence (valued at $12B, used by 40%+ of US physicians) built a clinical AI platform by:
1. Starting with open-access content (PubMed)
2. Licensing premium content from NEJM, JAMA, Wiley, Cochrane
3. Building a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) engine that searches 35M+ papers
4. Offering free access to physicians — monetizing via pharma advertising

**Key insight:** Reading licenses ≠ AI licenses. OpenEvidence paid millions SEPARATELY for AI rights on top of existing subscription access.

### Waraqa's AI Licensing Strategy (Three Phases)

```
Phase 1 (Now — Q4 2026): BUILD ON OWNED + OPEN CONTENT
├── Waraqa journal content (owned 100%)
├── Nama CRO research output (owned 100%)
├── Saudi clinical guidelines (MOH/SCFHS — government/open)
├── PubMed / PMC open access (35M+ articles)
├── SFDA drug data (government/open)
├── Preprints from bioRxiv/medRxiv (open)
└── Result: MVP of Saudi Evidence AI Platform (TTG-CII)

Phase 2 (2027): GOVERNMENT NEGOTIATION
├── Approach MOE/SDL: "Expand existing publisher licenses to include AI rights"
├── Argument: "You pay 80M+ for reading. Add 15-20% for AI rights.
│   Result: sovereign clinical AI infrastructure — not dependent on
│   American platforms like OpenEvidence"
├── Waraqa/CII operates the AI layer on behalf of the government
└── Result: Full content coverage with legal AI rights

Phase 3 (2027+): SELL SUBSCRIPTIONS
├── Hospitals (280+ MOH + 400+ private) → SAR 100K-500K/year each
├── Universities (30+) → SAR 150K-400K/year each
├── Research centers (50+) → SAR 50K-150K/year each
├── Direct institutional sales (bypassing SDL pricing)
└── Result: SAR 100M+ addressable market
```

### Why Waraqa Has a Unique Advantage

| OpenEvidence (USA) | Waraqa + TTG-CII (Saudi) |
|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Pays $M/year to license content | Owns content via TKG ecosystem (zero cost) |
| English only | Arabic + English |
| US clinical context | Saudi context (SFDA, MOH, SCFHS protocols) |
| No local regulatory alignment | Direct SCFHS/CCHI/MOH relationships |
| $12B valuation built on licensed content | Sovereign content = higher margins |
