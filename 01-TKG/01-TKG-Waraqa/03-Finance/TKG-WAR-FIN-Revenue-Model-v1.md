## 1. REVENUE ARCHITECTURE

**Source:** Article Processing Charges (APC), institutional subscriptions, pharma advertising

| Component | Mechanism | Waraqa Share |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|

### Stream 7: AI Content Licensing (NEW — March 2026)

**The highest-margin, highest-growth opportunity in Waraqa's portfolio.**

The global publishing industry has created a new revenue category: selling content rights specifically for AI training, fine-tuning, and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems. Wiley earned $44M in 2024 from AI licensing alone (+1,500% growth). OpenEvidence ($12B valuation) pays millions annually to license NEJM, JAMA, Wiley, and Cochrane content for its clinical AI.

**Waraqa's unique position:** Waraqa OWNS its content IP — no need to license from others. Revenue is pure margin.

**Two sub-streams:**

#### 7A: License Waraqa Content to External AI Platforms

| Buyer Type | What They License | Pricing Model |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| International AI platforms (OpenEvidence, UpToDate, etc.) | Saudi clinical guidelines, Arabic medical content, society journal articles | Annual license: $500K-2M |
| Regional AI companies | Arabic-language medical corpus | Per-article or corpus license |
| Pharma AI/analytics | Disease-specific research datasets | Per-dataset license |

#### 7B: Saudi Evidence AI Platform (with TTG-CII) — The Big Play

**Model:** Build a "Saudi OpenEvidence" — AI evidence discovery platform sold as B2B subscriptions to Saudi hospitals, universities, and research centers.

**Why this works:** The Saudi Digital Library (MOE) spends SAR 80-100M+ every 3 years buying reading subscriptions to foreign databases (Elsevier, Springer, Clarivate, EBSCO). Many hospitals and entities also buy directly from publishers, bypassing SDL due to perceived overpricing. The market is fragmented and underserved.

**Waraqa's role:** Content provider + co-operator with TTG-CII
**TTG-CII's role:** AI engine builder + platform operator
**Revenue split:** Defined per cross-group orchestration agreement

**Three-phase approach:**
- Phase 1: Build on owned content + open access (PubMed, SFDA, preprints) → zero licensing cost
- Phase 2: Negotiate AI rights expansion through government (MOE/SDL adds AI clause to existing publisher contracts)
- Phase 3: Sell B2B subscriptions directly to institutions

| Client Segment | Count | Price Range/Year | Addressable |
|---------------|-------|-----------------|-------------|
| MOH hospitals | 280+ | SAR 200K-500K | 56M-140M |
| Private hospitals | 400+ | SAR 100K-300K | 40M-120M |
| Universities | 30+ | SAR 150K-400K | 4.5M-12M |
| Research centers | 50+ | SAR 50K-150K | 2.5M-7.5M |
| **Total addressable** | | | **SAR 103M-280M** |

**Unit Economics — Stream 7 (Waraqa share only):**

| Metric | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 7A: External AI licensing | 0 | 500K | 2.0M |
| 7B: Saudi Evidence AI (Waraqa content royalty) | 0 | 200K | 3.0M |
| **Stream 7 total** | **0** | **700K** | **5.0M** |

> Note: Stream 7B revenue to Waraqa is the content royalty portion only.
> TTG-CII captures the platform subscription revenue separately.
> Combined CII + Waraqa revenue from Stream 7B could reach SAR 30M+ by Year 5.
