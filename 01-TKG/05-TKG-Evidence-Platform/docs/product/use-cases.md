## Use Case 1: Resident at 2 AM in the Emergency Department

**Persona:** Dr. Sara, PGY-2 Emergency Medicine resident, King Fahad Medical City

**Scenario:** A 67-year-old male presents with chest pain and atrial fibrillation. He has CKD stage 3b. Dr. Sara needs to decide on anticoagulation — but the standard guidelines don't address this specific combination clearly.

**Current reality:** She opens UpToDate on a slow hospital computer, searches in English, gets a generic international answer, can't find the Saudi Cardiology Society position, and makes a decision with uncertainty.

**With Saudi Evidence:**
1. Opens Saudi Evidence on her phone (WhatsApp-accessible or mobile web)
2. Types in Arabic or English: "anticoagulation AF with CKD stage 3b elderly"
3. Gets:
   - **Saudi Cardiology Society guideline** (if available) — highlighted first
   - **International guidelines** (ESC, AHA) with Saudi context notes
   - **Key studies** from PubMed/Embase with evidence grades
   - **Clinical decision pathway** from the MNC Decision Engine
4. Makes a confident, evidence-based decision
5. **0.25 CME hours** logged automatically to her Learning Passport
6. If no Saudi guideline exists → the question is flagged as an **evidence gap** → routed to PubHelper

**Ecosystem value:** Her question becomes data. Aggregated across thousands of residents, these questions reveal where Saudi guidelines are missing — feeding PubHelper's research pipeline and Waraqa's guideline development.

## Use Case 2: Consultant Preparing for Promotion

**Persona:** Dr. Khalid, Associate Professor of Endocrinology, King Saud University

**Scenario:** Dr. Khalid needs 3 more publications for full professor promotion. He has clinical insights but no time to manage research projects.

**With Saudi Evidence:**
1. Uses the **PubHelper intake** within Saudi Evidence to submit his research questions
2. PubHelper assigns a team: biostatistician, medical writer, methodology expert
3. His clinical questions become structured PICO formulations
4. Systematic review and meta-analysis conducted with his supervision
5. Published in the journal of HIS choice (not limited to Theeb journals) — targeting the best journal for his specific promotion pathway
6. Each publication feeds back into Saudi Evidence's knowledge graph
7. His **Learning Passport** tracks research CME credits
8. His **Physician Reputation Profile** (if subscribed) amplifies his expertise publicly

**Ecosystem value:** One professor's promotion generates 3+ publications for Waraqa/external journals, enriches the knowledge graph, and creates CME content from the research findings.

## Use Case 3: Hospital Seeking CBAHI Accreditation

**Persona:** Dr. Fatima, Quality Director at a 200-bed private hospital in Jeddah

**Scenario:** CBAHI surveyors are coming in 6 months. The hospital needs to demonstrate that clinicians have access to up-to-date scientific resources (Information Management standard) and that the hospital supports research activity.

**Current reality:** The hospital pays SAR 200K+ for UpToDate licenses, has scattered PDF guidelines on a shared drive, and research activity is minimal. Usage reports are hard to generate.

**With Saudi Evidence:**
1. Hospital signs an **institutional deployment** of Saudi Evidence (free in V1)
2. All 150 physicians get Saudi Evidence accounts linked to the hospital
3. **Institutional dashboard** shows:
   - Usage by department (who's accessing evidence, who's not)
   - Knowledge gap heatmaps (what questions are being asked, where guidelines are missing)
   - CME compliance rates per department
   - Research activity through PubHelper
4. For CBAHI surveyors, Dr. Fatima generates a **compliance report** showing:
   - 95% of clinicians actively accessing scientific resources (vs. 30% UpToDate usage)
   - Department-level evidence utilization dashboards
   - Active research projects through PubHelper
   - CME compliance tracking via Learning Passport

**Ecosystem value:** Every institutional deployment = pipeline for Nama Academy (CDI training, CME programs), Integra RCM (if the hospital needs revenue cycle support), and Welunion (if the hospital wants MSO services).

## Use Case 4: Saudi Scientific Society Upgrading to Category A

**Persona:** Dr. Mohammed, President of the Saudi Emergency Medicine Society (250 members)

**Scenario:** The society is Category C. They need a journal, more CME activities, a better website, research output, and community engagement — but they have no operational infrastructure.

**With Saudi Evidence:**
1. Society partners with Theeb (Waraqa + Nama Academy + Saudi Evidence)
2. **Saudi Evidence becomes the society's digital hub:**
   - Society-branded section within Saudi Evidence
   - Members access guidelines, CME, and research tools through one interface
   - Emergency medicine guidelines hosted as Live Guidelines (continuously updated)
3. **Waraqa launches the Saudi Journal of Emergency Medicine** (SJEM)
   - Peer-reviewed, indexed pathway, society keeps 40-60% of revenue
4. **Nama Academy delivers 4+ CME activities per quarter** under society brand
5. **PubHelper coordinates research:** 10 EM physicians conduct systematic reviews on priority clinical questions (chest pain pathways, sepsis management, etc.)
6. **Every activity is tracked:** membership growth, CME hours, publications, community engagement — all mapped to SCFHS scoring criteria

**Scoring impact:**

| SCFHS Category | Before | After Year 1 |
|---------------|--------|-------------|
| Activities & Conferences (450 pts) | 80 | 350+ |
| Research & Publications (260 pts) | 20 | 180+ |
| Community Service (240 pts) | 30 | 150+ |
| Digital Presence (90 pts) | 20 | 80+ |
| **Projected classification** | **Category C** | **Category A** |

## Use Case 5: Pharma Company Launching a Drug in KSA

**Persona:** Regional Medical Director at a multinational pharmaceutical company launching a new GLP-1 agonist for Type 2 Diabetes

**Scenario:** The company needs Saudi-specific clinical evidence, physician education, and guideline inclusion for market access.

**With Saudi Evidence (via Theeb Ecosystem orchestration):**
1. **Nama CRO** runs Phase IV observational study in Saudi diabetic patients
2. **PubHelper** coordinates systematic reviews comparing the drug to existing therapies
3. **Waraqa** publishes results in a Saudi diabetes journal
4. **Saudi Evidence** integrates the evidence into:
   - The diabetes Live Guideline page
   - MNC Decision Engine (medical necessity criteria for insurance approval)
   - CME micro-course on new GLP-1 therapies (Nama Academy, pharma-sponsored)
5. **VBH Co** measures real-world outcomes in Welunion CDM patients
6. Endocrinologists across Saudi Arabia see the drug positioned within local evidence context — not just global marketing

**Revenue flows:** The pharma company pays for CRO services, sponsored CME, and evidence positioning. Saudi Evidence is the distribution layer — free for physicians, valuable for the ecosystem.

## Use Case 6: WhatsApp Group to Structured Knowledge

**Persona:** Longevity EBM Clinicians group (300+ experts discussing evidence daily)

**Scenario:** Every day, experts share studies, debate interventions, and discuss clinical experiences. But all knowledge disappears in chat scroll. No structured output. No guideline impact.

**With Saudi Evidence (Longevity Track):**
1. A **Longevity Evidence Forum** is created within Saudi Evidence
2. Key discussions from the WhatsApp group are captured and structured:
   - Topic tagged (fasting, biomarkers, supplements, exercise protocols)
   - Evidence level assessed
   - References indexed into the knowledge graph
3. Recurring questions become **PubHelper research projects**:
   - "Is rapamycin effective for longevity in humans?" → systematic review
   - "What biomarkers predict biological age?" → meta-analysis
4. Results become **Live Guidelines** for longevity medicine
5. Physicians earn CME credits for contributing to structured discussions
6. Over time, the forum replaces WhatsApp as the primary knowledge-sharing platform — because every contribution becomes a permanent, searchable, citable evidence asset
