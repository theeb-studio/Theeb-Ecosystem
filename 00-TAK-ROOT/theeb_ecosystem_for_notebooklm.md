# Theeb Ecosystem — Full Overview
**Prepared for NotebookLM | March 2026 | Confidential**

---

## 1. What is Theeb?

- Theeb is a **healthcare ecosystem** built in Saudi Arabia — a group of specialized companies that work independently but collaborate on large projects.
- It is organized into **three strategic groups**: one focused on medical knowledge and research (TKG), one focused on financial intelligence and value-based healthcare (TVG), and one focused on delivering care directly to patients (THDG).
- The ecosystem is **not one company** — it is a network of 11+ distinct companies that can be assembled in any combination to serve a client, a hospital, a pharmaceutical company, or a scientific society.
- The core insight is that **no single competitor can replicate the full combination** — a pharma company can hire a research organization, but they cannot hire an entire ecosystem.
- Every company inside Theeb has its own legal identity, licenses, and P&L — but they share strategy, quality standards, and data infrastructure.

## 2. Core Components

### Group 1 — TKG: Theeb Knowledge Group
*The scientific engine of the ecosystem*

- **Waraqa** — a medical publishing company that launches peer-reviewed journals for scientific societies and develops clinical guidelines
- **Nama CRO** — a clinical research organization that designs and runs studies (Phase I–IV, real-world evidence, disease registries)
- **Nama Academy** — a healthcare training academy with SCFHS accreditation, delivering CME courses, life support certifications (BLS/ACLS/ATLS), and simulation-based training
- **Nama Bioequivalence Center** — a specialized laboratory for testing generic drug equivalence against branded drugs
- **Evidence Platform** — a digital knowledge platform providing clinical decision support, CME tracking, and real-world data analytics for physicians and institutions

### Group 2 — TVG: Theeb Value Group
*The financial and analytics intelligence layer*

- **Integra RCM** — a revenue cycle management company that helps hospitals collect what they are owed from insurers (billing, coding, AI-powered claims)
- **MGA Company (Nexus MGA)** — a managing general agent that designs insurance products, including a unique malpractice package bundled with CME education hours
- **VBH Company** — a value-based healthcare company that measures patient outcomes, models cost-of-care, and designs contracts that pay providers based on results rather than volume

### Group 3 — THDG: Theeb Health Delivery Group
*The patient-facing delivery layer*

- **Welunion** — the flagship healthcare platform combining physical clinics, digital telehealth, and chronic disease management under one brand
- **Integrative Health (IHH)** — an operating company transitioning into Welunion, currently running home care, CDM programs, and clinical services
- **TAP Pharmacy** — an in-clinic dispensing network capturing prescription revenue at the point of care (currently 2 Riyadh locations, expanding nationally)
- **Clinical Ventures** — flexible partnerships in mental health, pediatrics, and specialty care (owned fully, partially, or managed on behalf of partners)

## 3. How Everything Connects

- When a **pharmaceutical company** wants to launch a drug in Saudi Arabia, Theeb assembles: Nama CRO (run the trial) + Evidence Platform (analyze real-world data) + Waraqa (publish the results) + Nama Academy (train physicians) + Welunion clinics (deliver the drug to patients) + VBH Company (prove cost-effectiveness to insurers) — all in one coordinated engagement.
- When a **scientific society** with no resources approaches Theeb, Waraqa launches their journal, Nama Academy runs their CME conferences, the Evidence Platform hosts their digital hub, and Nama CRO connects their doctors to research projects — and the society receives 40–60% of all revenue generated in their name.
- When a **hospital network** wants to improve financially and clinically, Integra RCM fixes their billing, VBH Company measures their outcomes, Nama Academy trains their staff, and Welunion manages their outpatient services — after 18 months, the hospital is embedded across all Theeb layers.
- The **lead entity rule** applies to every project: whichever company is closest to the client's primary need takes the lead and coordinates the others — the client always has one point of contact.
- **Revenue is pre-agreed** before any project begins — each company invoices separately for its scope, and the client receives one integrated output.

## 4. Key Features

- **Entity independence with operational interoperability** — every company can survive and grow on its own, but together they create solutions no competitor can replicate.
- **Asset-light clinical infrastructure** — Welunion rents simulation centers (abundant and underutilized in KSA), uses home care fleets for pharmacy delivery, and leverages existing SCFHS accreditation rather than building from scratch.
- **Revenue sharing over fixed fees** — scientific societies keep 40–60% of journal revenue and 40–50% of CME revenue; Theeb provides all the infrastructure and operations at zero upfront cost to the society.
- **A four-layer financial intelligence stack** — from billing (Layer 1) to risk design (Layer 2) to outcomes measurement (Layer 3) to outcome-based contracts (Layer 4) — TVG is the only company in Saudi Arabia building all four layers simultaneously.
- **Clinician-led credibility** — key leaders are practicing clinicians and professors (e.g., the Nama Academy GM leads the Saudi Heart Association), giving Theeb peer-level access to scientific societies, hospitals, and government bodies.

## 5. Important Numbers & Metrics

**The Market**
- Saudi Arabia spends **SAR 196 billion ($52.2B)** on healthcare annually
- The addressable market for Theeb's primary care and chronic disease services is **SAR 16.8 billion**
- Vision 2030 is privatizing **2,300 primary health centers** — a structural opening for Welunion

**THDG / Welunion Growth**
- Year 1: 11 clinics, SAR 20M revenue (–SAR 10M EBITDA while building)
- Year 5: 71 clinics, **SAR 479M revenue, ~42% EBITDA margin**
- Each clinic reaches **operating breakeven in 7 months**
- Buildout cost per clinic: **SAR 500,000**
- Home Care: **265,800 annual visits, 61% EBITDA**
- Chronic Disease Management: **10,250 enrolled members, SAR 50.6M annual revenue**

**TKG / Nama Academy**
- Saudi Arabia has **80+ scientific societies** — all are target partners
- SCFHS requires every licensed health professional to earn **30 CME credits/year**
- The MGA malpractice + CME bundle is priced at **SAR 800–1,200 per physician per year**
- A scientific society partnering with Theeb can move from Category C to **Category A (1,150+ SCFHS points)**

**TVG**
- Hospital insurer loss ratios in KSA are running at **83–96%** — driving demand for RCM and VBP solutions
- AR-DRG (new hospital payment system) is being implemented by CHI in **2026** — Integra RCM is positioned as the readiness partner
