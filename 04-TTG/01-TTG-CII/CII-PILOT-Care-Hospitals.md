# CII PILOT PROGRAM — CARE HOSPITALS (PHASE 1)
**Entity: TTG-CII | Project Code: CII-PLT-01**
**Status: Pre-deployment | Duration: 3–6 months**
**Classification: Confidential — Strategic**

---

## 1. Overview

This pilot represents the **first real-world deployment of CII** (Clinical Intelligence Infrastructure) — the sovereign clinical intelligence platform developed by Theeb Technology Group (TTG).

CII is not a clinical application. It is an **intelligence layer that sits above healthcare systems**, transforming clinical questions, physician workflows, and patient encounter data into continuous medical knowledge, structured clinical evidence, and real-world data assets.

The Care Hospitals pilot is the proving ground — where CII moves from architecture to reality.

---

## 2. Objective

To validate CII across five dimensions:

| Dimension | What We Prove |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Physician Adoption** | Physicians use CII daily — it becomes workflow, not an add-on |
| **Clinical Usefulness** | CII improves diagnostic reasoning, evidence retrieval speed, and documentation quality |
| **Knowledge Generation** | CII produces structured clinical knowledge — not just answers, but publishable, guideline-grade outputs |
| **Real-World Data Creation** | CII generates de-identified clinical datasets from physician interactions — the raw material for RWE |
| **Economic Viability** | The unit economics of CII (per physician, per hospital) are validated for scale |

---

## 3. Why Care Hospitals

Care Hospitals was selected as the Phase 1 pilot site based on five criteria:

| Criterion | Care Hospitals Fit |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Multi-hospital system** | Multiple facilities — tests CII across different clinical environments and specialties |
| **High patient volume** | Sufficient encounter volume to generate meaningful data within 3–6 months |
| **Research infrastructure** | Established IRB, research committees, and data governance — enables rapid ethical approval |
| **Publishing ecosystem** | Active relationship with TKG (Waraqa, scientific societies) — pilot outputs can be published |
| **Physician engagement** | Clinical staff with demonstrated interest in evidence-based practice and CME participation |

> **This pilot is not a test environment.** CII is deployed into a live healthcare system with real patients, real physicians, and real clinical decisions.

## 4. Entry Point — Clinical Evidence Assistant

The CII deployment begins with the **Clinical Evidence Assistant** — the same entry point defined in the CII platform strategy:

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **User** | Physicians — directly, at the point of care |
| **Integration** | No EHR integration required (browser-based or standalone app) |
| **Workflow** | Embeds immediately into existing clinical workflow — zero disruption |
| **Language** | Arabic and English clinical queries supported |
| **Function** | Evidence retrieval, differential support, drug interaction screening, documentation assistance |

**Why this entry point:** It requires no IT infrastructure changes at Care Hospitals. Physicians can start using it on day one. The data flywheel begins immediately.

## 6. Scientific Governance Model

CII does not operate as an unsupervised AI tool. Every clinical domain within the pilot is governed by a structured scientific authority:

```
Clinical Domain (e.g., Cardiology, Endocrinology, Surgery)
    ↓
Saudi Scientific Society (e.g., Saudi Heart Association)
    ↓
Affiliated Journal (e.g., via TKG Waraqa)
    ↓
Editorial Board / Clinical Review Committee
    ↓
Validated CII Knowledge Output
```

**This governance ensures:**
- Scientific validation of CII recommendations
- Alignment with Saudi and international clinical guidelines
- Structured knowledge creation that can be published, cited, and referenced
- Clinical credibility that no foreign AI platform can replicate — because foreign platforms do not have Saudi scientific society endorsement

## 7. Incentive Model — Physician Contribution System

CII introduces a **contribution-based incentive system** that rewards physicians for clinical intelligence activities:

### Token Generation

| Activity | Tokens Earned |
|----------|--------------|
| Clinical question submitted with structured context | Standard |
| Evidence review and quality rating | Enhanced |
| Research contribution (case report, cohort identification) | Premium |
| Guideline contribution (draft recommendation for society review) | Premium+ |
| Peer review of another physician's contribution | Standard |

### Token Redemption

| Reward | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **CME credits** | Accredited through SCFHS via TKG Nama Academy — direct integration |
| **Academic recognition** | Contributions credited in published guidelines, systematic reviews |
| **Financial rewards** | Token-to-payment conversion for high-value contributions |
| **Conference access** | Priority access to TKG-organized scientific society conferences |

**Strategic value of this model:** It creates a self-reinforcing knowledge loop — physicians contribute clinical intelligence, receive professional rewards, and generate the structured data that makes CII more valuable over time. This is the human layer of the data flywheel.

## 9. Revenue Model During Pilot

| Element | Terms |
|---------|-------|
| **CII access fee to Care Hospitals** | Zero during pilot period (3–6 months) |
| **Value exchange** | Care Hospitals provides: physician access, clinical environment, data rights (de-identified), co-publication rights |
| **Post-pilot pricing** | First-refusal on enterprise license at preferred pricing (pre-agreed range) |
| **Data ownership** | All data generated during pilot belongs to TTG-CII. Care Hospitals retains right to use de-identified aggregate reports for internal quality improvement |

> **Principle:** The pilot is free in exchange for access and data. Post-pilot, Care Hospitals becomes a paying enterprise client — with preferential terms as the founding deployment partner.

## 10. Intellectual Property — Ownership Structure

| IP Category | Owner | Notes |
|------------|-------|-------|

## 13. Exit Conditions

### If Pilot Succeeds
- Care Hospitals converts to enterprise client (paid license)
- CII platform validated — Phase 2 expansion to additional health systems
- RWE datasets and clinical knowledge assets established as TTG-CII property
- Regulatory submissions initiated

### If Pilot Fails
- TTG-CII retains all IP, data, knowledge, and models generated during pilot
- Care Hospitals retains no proprietary CII assets
- Third-party technology components returned or license terminated
- Lessons documented; platform iterated before next deployment attempt

> **Either way, CII owns everything it built.** The pilot is designed so that failure is a learning event, not a loss of assets.
