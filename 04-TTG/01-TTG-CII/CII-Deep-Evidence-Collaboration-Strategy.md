# CII × DEEP EVIDENCE — PHASED COLLABORATION STRATEGY
**Entity: TTG-CII | Project Code: CII-COL-01**
**Status: Negotiation | Classification: Confidential — Strategic**

---

## 1. Strategic Principle

> **Use → Integrate → Localize → Own**

Deep Evidence is an **accelerator** — not a dependency. CII uses Deep Evidence's existing AI engine to accelerate initial deployment, then systematically transitions to a fully localized, fully owned clinical intelligence infrastructure.

At the end of this collaboration, one of two outcomes is acceptable:
1. Deep Evidence is fully integrated into CII as a licensed technology component — operating under CII's brand, governance, and data ownership
2. Deep Evidence is replaced entirely by CII's own model stack — built on the data, knowledge, and clinical models generated during the collaboration

**Both outcomes leave CII sovereign. Both outcomes leave TTG owning the IP.**

---

## 2. Objective

To accelerate CII's market entry by leveraging Deep Evidence's existing clinical AI capabilities — while ensuring:

- **IP ownership** transfers to or originates with TTG-CII for all derivative works
- **Brand identity** remains exclusively CII at every external touchpoint
- **Data sovereignty** is absolute — no CII-generated data flows to Deep Evidence without explicit TTG consent
- **Transition pathway** is contractually defined and time-bound — CII is never locked in
- **Competitive protection** prevents Deep Evidence from deploying competing infrastructure in KSA/GCC during the collaboration

## 3. Phased Transition Plan

### Phase 1 — Deployment (Month 0–6)

**Deep Evidence used as-is within CII pilot at Care Hospitals:**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Role** | Deep Evidence engine powers the Clinical Evidence Assistant inside CII |
| **Visibility** | Zero. Deep Evidence does not appear in any user interface, hospital agreement, or published output |
| **Data flow** | Physician queries processed by Deep Evidence engine → responses delivered through CII interface → all interaction data stored in CII infrastructure |
| **Brand** | CII only. Physicians, hospital staff, and scientific societies interact with CII exclusively |
| **IP** | All CII-side adaptations (Arabic models, Saudi guideline integration, prompt engineering, clinical taxonomies) belong to TTG-CII from day one |

**Key deliverable:** Working CII deployment at Care Hospitals, live physician usage, data flywheel initiated.

### Phase 2 — Scientific Governance Integration (Month 3–9)

**Deep Evidence outputs brought under Saudi scientific society governance:**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Governance layer** | Saudi scientific societies review and validate CII/Deep Evidence clinical recommendations |
| **Guideline alignment** | Deep Evidence outputs calibrated against Saudi-adapted clinical guidelines (managed by TKG Waraqa) |
| **Editorial oversight** | Society editorial boards approve clinical knowledge outputs before they enter CII's published knowledge base |
| **Knowledge creation** | Structured clinical knowledge begins to accumulate — owned by CII, reviewed by societies |

**What this achieves:** CII's clinical output gains the credibility stamp that no AI engine — including Deep Evidence — can provide independently. Saudi scientific society endorsement is the moat.

### Phase 3 — Local Knowledge Integration (Month 6–12)

**CII evidence layer enriched with Saudi-specific clinical knowledge:**

| Source | Integration Path |
|--------|-----------------|
| Saudi journals (via TKG Waraqa) | Direct ingestion into CII evidence layer |

**What this achieves:** CII's evidence layer becomes increasingly Saudi-specific. The value of Deep Evidence's generic Western engine decreases proportionally. CII's own knowledge base becomes the primary source of clinical intelligence.

---

### Phase 4 — Closed Knowledge Access (Month 9–15)

**CII gains access to closed-source scientific databases:**

| Partner | Access Gained |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ministry of Education** | Institutional journal subscriptions available to CII's evidence layer |
| **Saudi Digital Library** | Licensed access to subscription-based medical journals and clinical databases |
| **Publisher agreements** | Direct licensing from Elsevier, Springer, Wiley for CII evidence ingestion (negotiated as national infrastructure, not individual subscription) |

**What this achieves:** CII's evidence layer becomes comprehensive — not just open-access journals but the full corpus of medical literature. This is a capability that Deep Evidence provides generically; CII now replicates it with sovereign access and local curation.

---

### Phase 5 — CII Integration (Month 12–18)

**Deep Evidence formally integrated as a component inside CII — or replaced:**

**Path A — Integration:**
- Deep Evidence engine licensed permanently to CII under white-label agreement
- Deep Evidence operates as an invisible technology component inside CII Layer 1+2
- All external-facing aspects (brand, governance, data, IP) remain CII
- License terms: fixed annual fee, no per-query pricing, no data access for Deep Evidence
- CII retains right to replace the engine at any time

**Path B — Replacement:**
- CII's own model stack (built on 12–18 months of Saudi clinical data, Arabic language models, and local guideline training) reaches parity with Deep Evidence
- Deep Evidence license terminated
- CII operates fully independently on its own AI infrastructure
- All models trained on CII-owned data remain CII property

**Decision point:** CII leadership evaluates at month 12. If CII's own models achieve ≥90% clinical accuracy parity with Deep Evidence, Path B is preferred. If not, Path A — with renegotiated terms reflecting CII's reduced dependency.

| Output | Market |

### Phase 7 — Long-Term Strategic Position (Month 24+)

**Two acceptable end states:**

| End State | Description | CII Position |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **A — Deep integration** | Deep Evidence operates as a permanent, licensed component inside CII's technology stack | CII owns the platform, brand, data, and all derivative IP. Deep Evidence earns a technology license fee. |
| **B — Full independence** | CII operates entirely on its own AI models, with no Deep Evidence dependency | CII owns everything. Deep Evidence relationship ends cleanly or converts to advisory. |

**Both end states are acceptable.** The collaboration is designed so that CII arrives at sovereignty regardless of which path materializes.

## 4. IP Ownership Framework

This is the most critical section of the collaboration agreement.

### IP Layering

| Layer | Description | Owner |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| **A — Deep Evidence base engine** | Pre-existing AI model, training data, and core algorithms | Deep Evidence |
| **B — CII adaptations** | Arabic clinical models, Saudi guideline integration, prompt templates, clinical taxonomies, physician interaction models, UI/UX | **TTG-CII** |
| **C — Generated data** | Physician queries, interaction patterns, clinical knowledge gaps, RWE datasets, structured knowledge entries | **TTG-CII** |
| **D — Published outputs** | Clinical guidelines, systematic reviews, case reports, evidence packages | **TTG-CII** (with co-authorship as applicable) |
| **E — Derivative models** | Any AI models fine-tuned on CII data, trained on CII interactions, or adapted for CII use cases | **TTG-CII** |

### Non-Negotiable IP Clauses

1. **All derivative works belong to TTG-CII.** Any model, dataset, taxonomy, or clinical output created during or as a result of the collaboration is TTG-CII property — regardless of whether Deep Evidence technology contributed to its creation.

2. **No training on CII data without consent.** Deep Evidence may not use CII-generated data (queries, interactions, outcomes) to train, improve, or benchmark its own models — unless TTG-CII provides explicit written consent with defined commercial terms.

3. **IP transfer at termination.** Upon termination of the collaboration, all Layer B, C, D, and E IP remains with TTG-CII. Deep Evidence retains only Layer A (its pre-existing engine). No residual rights, no reversionary claims.

4. **Source code escrow.** Deep Evidence deposits its engine source code in escrow. If Deep Evidence ceases operations, is acquired, or breaches the agreement, TTG-CII gains access to the escrowed source code to ensure operational continuity.

## 6. Competitive Protection

| Clause | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Geographic exclusivity** | During the collaboration (and 12 months after termination), Deep Evidence will not deploy, license, or support competing clinical intelligence infrastructure in Saudi Arabia or GCC markets through any other partner |
| **Vertical exclusivity** | Deep Evidence will not provide its engine to any Saudi healthcare platform that competes with CII's defined market (clinical decision support, medical documentation AI, RWE generation) |
| **Data non-compete** | Deep Evidence will not use knowledge of CII's clinical data structure, physician interaction patterns, or guideline integration methods to develop competing products |

---

## 7. Dependency Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| **Deep Evidence raises prices** | Cap on annual license fee escalation (CPI + 3% max); CII retains right to replace at any time |
| **Deep Evidence is acquired** | Change-of-control clause: TTG-CII may terminate immediately and retain all Layer B–E IP; source code escrow activates |
| **Deep Evidence pivots away from healthcare** | Same as acquisition clause — operational continuity guaranteed through escrow |
| **Deep Evidence underperforms** | SLA with defined clinical accuracy benchmarks; breach triggers replacement right |
| **Deep Evidence demands data access** | All data clauses are non-negotiable. Any attempt to access CII data outside the defined scope triggers breach notification |
