## Group Identity

TTG builds the **intelligence infrastructure that the Theeb Ecosystem — and Saudi healthcare — runs on.**

TTG is not a product company. It is not an app studio. It is the technology infrastructure group — building the AI layers, data platforms, and clinical intelligence systems that every other group in the ecosystem depends on, and that external health systems, insurers, and government entities will pay to access.

TTG applies the infrastructure narrative to healthcare:
- **AWS** did not build websites — it built the layer that websites run on.
- **Nvidia** did not build AI — it built the infrastructure that AI runs on.
- **TTG** does not build clinics or journals — it builds the intelligence layer that clinics, journals, and health systems run on.

TTG is the **intelligence operating system** of Saudi healthcare.

## TTG as an Independent Company

CII (and TTG broadly) is structured as an **independent technology company** — not a division of TKG, TVG, or THDG. This is deliberate:

- **External clients:** CII can serve competing health systems and government entities without conflict of interest
- **Independent capital:** TTG can raise external investment (venture, PIF, STC, SVC) on its own merits
- **Clean IP:** Models, data, and platform IP belong to TTG — licensed to the three groups under defined agreements
- **Credibility:** An independent clinical AI company carries more weight with health ministry and hospital group buyers than a product division of a service company
