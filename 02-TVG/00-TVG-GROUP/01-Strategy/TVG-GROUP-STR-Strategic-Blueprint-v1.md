## PART ONE — GROUP IDENTITY

### What Theeb Value Group Is

Theeb Value Group (TVG) is a **healthcare infrastructure platform company**.

It does not deliver clinical care. It does not sell consulting engagements. It builds the **financial, data, and risk infrastructure** that connects healthcare providers, insurance payers, and regulators into a coherent, outcome-oriented system.

TVG operates on the premise that value-based healthcare does not fail because of clinical resistance — it fails because the **infrastructure prerequisite layers** are missing. Hospitals cannot accept outcome-based contracts without accurate revenue data. Insurers cannot design risk-sharing models without provider performance intelligence. Regulators cannot enforce value-based procurement without a measurement layer that doesn't yet exist.

TVG builds those layers — in sequence, with each layer funding and enabling the next.

### What Theeb Value Group Is Not

| It is NOT | It IS |
|-----------|-------|
| A consulting firm | A platform and infrastructure company |
| A technology vendor | A system integrator and data layer builder |
| A payer or insurer | The infrastructure that connects payers and providers |
| A clinical operator | The financial and data intelligence layer above clinical operations |
| A project-based business | A recurring-revenue platform with compounding network effects |

### The Core Insight

Saudi Arabia is building value-based healthcare policy from the top down — CHI bundled payments, AR-DRG implementation, CCHI PROMs mandates, regulatory sandbox programs. But the **execution infrastructure** does not exist at scale.

TVG's strategic position is to build that execution infrastructure before it becomes commoditized — and to own the data layer that makes all value-based contracting possible.

### LAYER 1 — REVENUE INFRASTRUCTURE
**Existing Asset: Integra RCM**
**Timeline: Active — 2024 onwards**

#### What It Does
Layer 1 is the financial foundation of the entire architecture. It sits inside the provider's revenue cycle — coding, claims submission, denial management, and revenue optimization — and generates the **first structured data set** about what clinical encounters actually cost and how they're being reimbursed.

#### Core Capabilities
- **AI-Assisted Clinical Coding** — ICD-10, CPT, SNOMED, and AR-DRG coding with NLP-driven CDI (Clinical Documentation Improvement)
- **Claims Management** — Submission, tracking, and automated re-submission workflows
- **Denial Analytics** — Pattern recognition across denial reasons to identify systemic revenue leakage
- **Revenue Intelligence Dashboard** — Real-time financial performance benchmarked against regional norms
- **AR-DRG Readiness** — Pre-positioning providers for the 2026 AR-DRG implementation with DRG grouper tools and revenue impact modeling

#### Why It Comes First
1. **Trust entry point** — Providers invite Layer 1 because it solves an immediate, painful financial problem
2. **Data acquisition** — Every coded claim produces structured clinical-financial data that feeds Layers 3 and 4
3. **Relationship asset** — Deep integration with provider billing and HIS systems creates switching costs
4. **Cash generation** — Recurring revenue from performance-based RCM contracts funds Layer 2 development

#### Revenue Model
- Percentage of net collections (2–4%)
- Fixed monthly platform fee + per-claim processing
- Performance-based bonuses on denial recovery

#### Strategic Output for Upper Layers
→ Claim-level cost data (input to Layer 3 cost analytics)
→ DRG distribution across provider network (input to Layer 4 bundle pricing)
→ Payer mix and contract terms (input to Layer 2 underwriting)

### LAYER 2 — RISK INFRASTRUCTURE
**Entity: TVG-MGA (Managing General Agent)**
**Timeline: 2026–2027 — Regulatory licensing phase**

#### What It Does
Layer 2 introduces the **payer-side infrastructure**. A Managing General Agent (MGA) sits between insurance companies and healthcare providers, designing and managing the risk products that traditional insurers lack the clinical intelligence to build.

TVG's MGA is not a conventional MGA. It is a **data-driven risk platform** that uses Layer 1 clinical-financial data to underwrite healthcare risk more accurately than any traditional actuarial model can.

#### Core Capabilities
- **Underwriting Analytics Engine** — Risk stratification models built on Layer 1 encounter data
- **Alternative Risk Products** — Capitation, bundled-episode insurance, employer self-insurance frameworks
- **Provider-Payer Integration Layer** — API-based connectivity between provider RCM systems (Layer 1) and payer claims systems
- **Population Health Risk Scoring** — Individual and cohort risk models for chronic disease, maternal health, mental health
- **Longevity and Preventive Benefits Design** — Employer benefit packages tied to outcomes, not utilization

#### The MGA Structural Advantage
Traditional insurers price on historical utilization with no real-time clinical intelligence. TVG's MGA prices on:
1. Real provider cost data (from Layer 1)
2. Outcome probability models (from Layer 3)
3. Payer-specific portfolio risk (from its own MGA book)

This creates a **structural underwriting advantage** — TVG can offer fairer prices to providers and better margins to insurer partners because its data is more accurate.

#### Regulatory Pathway (KSA)
- **SAMA Insurance License** / MGA authorization required
- **Regulatory Sandbox** application prepared (see `SHR-REG-Regulatory-Sandbox-Application-EN-v1.pdf`)
- Target: Full MGA authorization by Q3 2027

#### Revenue Model
- Underwriting commissions from insurance carrier partners (15–20% of premium)
- Administration fees on self-insured employer schemes
- Risk-sharing arrangements with carriers (profit commission on favorable loss ratios)

#### Strategic Output for Upper Layers
→ Insurance product frameworks designed for bundled episode payment
→ Risk-stratified patient populations (input to Layer 3 population health)
→ Payer cost benchmarks (input to Layer 4 contract pricing)

### LAYER 3 — VALUE INTELLIGENCE
**Entity: TVG-VBH (VBH Co Platform — Intelligence Module)**
**Timeline: 2026–2028 — Parallel to Layer 2**

#### What It Does
Layer 3 is the **measurement engine** of the TVG platform. It aggregates data from Layers 1 and 2, adds patient-reported outcome data (PROMs), and produces the intelligence that makes outcome-based contracting possible.

Without Layer 3, value-based contracts are theoretical. With it, contracts become mathematically defensible: outcomes are defined, baselines are established, and performance is measured against agreed benchmarks.

#### Core Capabilities

**Cost-of-Care Analytics**
- Episode-level total cost (provider + pharmacy + diagnostics)
- DRG-level cost benchmarking across the provider network
- Cost variation analysis: identifies high-cost outliers and low-cost exemplars

**Patient Outcome Tracking (PROMs)**
- Validated outcome instruments: ICHOM sets, disease-specific (PHQ-9, HbA1c targets, EQ-5D, WOMAC, PROMIS)
- Digital PROMs collection via patient-facing app, SMS, or tablet at point of care
- CCHI-compliant PROMs data model (aligned with CCHI mandate)
- Outcome benchmarking by provider, by condition, by payer

**Population Health Analytics**
- Chronic disease registry (diabetes, hypertension, COPD, mental health)
- Risk stratification: identify high-risk patients before costly episodes
- Preventive care gap analysis (missed screenings, non-adherent patients)
- Cohort outcome reporting for employer and payer clients

**Provider Performance Intelligence**
- Quality scorecard per provider per condition
- Readmission rates, complication rates, adherence to care pathways
- AR-DRG performance: actual DRG weight vs. expected for case mix
- Comparative effectiveness across the TVG network

**The AR-DRG Intelligence Module**
- DRG grouper integrated with Layer 1 coding output
- Case-mix index tracking per hospital
- Revenue impact modeling for proposed DRG tariff changes
- Preparation for CHI bundled payment reporting

#### Data Architecture
- **Data sources**: Layer 1 claims, Layer 2 payer data, PROMs collection, HIE feeds, pharmacy (TAP), lab
- **Integration**: HL7 FHIR APIs — no HIS replacement, overlay integration only
- **Privacy**: De-identified at rest; pseudonymized for analytics; re-identified only for direct care
- **Governance**: Data stays in KSA; provider retains ownership; TVG holds analytics license

#### Revenue Model
- SaaS subscription per provider (tiered by bed count / encounter volume)
- Outcome measurement-as-a-service for payer clients
- Population health program fees from employers and self-insured entities
- Regulatory reporting services (CCHI PROMs submission)

#### Strategic Output for Layer 4
→ Condition-specific outcome benchmarks (basis for bundle pricing)
→ Provider quality scores (input to contract eligibility and tier assignment)
→ Risk-adjusted cost baselines (input to shared savings calculation)

### LAYER 4 — OUTCOME-BASED CONTRACTING PLATFORMS
**Entity: TVG-VBH (VBH Co Platform — Contracting Module)**
**Timeline: 2028+ — When Layers 1–3 are established**

#### What It Does
Layer 4 is the **economic execution layer** — the place where value-based contracts are designed, priced, monitored, and settled. It sits above NPHIES and the existing fee-for-service infrastructure, using data from all lower layers to make alternative payment models financially viable.

TVG does not replace NPHIES. It operates **above NPHIES** — using NPHIES claims as a data input and adding the outcome, risk, and analytics layers that NPHIES does not provide.

#### Core Capabilities

**Bundled Payment Engine**
- Condition-specific bundle catalog (aligned with CHI's 7 priority conditions: AMI, hip/knee replacement, diabetes management, maternity, COPD, mental health, oncology)
- Bundle pricing: risk-adjusted, benchmarked against Layer 3 cost data
- Attribution: assigns patient episodes to responsible provider using NPHIES + PROMs data
- Reconciliation: calculates payout vs. bundle target at episode close

**Shared Savings Contract Platform**
- Provider performance measurement against quality benchmarks
- Savings calculation: actual cost vs. risk-adjusted target
- Distribution rules: savings shared between provider network and payer per contract terms
- Gainsharing models for multi-specialty care teams

**Value-Based Procurement (for Employers and the State)**
- Employer-facing contracts: premium tied to defined health outcomes (HbA1c improvement, BMI reduction, readmission avoidance)
- CNHI/CHI procurement alignment: TVG's contract engine outputs reports in the format required by national regulatory bodies
- Government-facing frameworks: bundled payment proposals for MOH or NCHI programs

**Outcome-Based Provider Contracts**
- Performance tiers: Bronze / Silver / Gold provider classification by condition
- Pay-for-performance add-ons above base DRG tariff
- Network design: steerage of patients toward higher-performing providers

**Contract Intelligence**
- Simulation engine: models financial impact of proposed contract terms before signing
- In-contract monitoring: real-time dashboard showing performance vs. targets
- Dispute resolution: data-driven adjudication of contested episodes

#### Revenue Model
- Transaction fee on each settled episode (0.5–1.5% of bundle value)
- Annual contract management fee per payer client
- Shared upside: TVG earns a share of savings generated (10–20% of savings pool)
- Regulatory reporting and compliance fee

### Provider Integration Model
TVG integrates with providers through:
1. **Layer 1 RCM team** — embedded in or adjacent to provider billing department
2. **FHIR API integration** — connects to existing HIS (Cerner, SAP, Aramco HIS, Oracle) without replacement
3. **PROMs collection point** — patient-facing touchpoints at discharge, follow-up
4. **Dashboard portal** — provider accesses their performance data in real time

TVG does NOT require hospitals to replace their HIS. It sits as a **data and analytics layer on top** of whatever system they already use.

### Payer Integration Model
TVG connects to payers through:
1. **MGA relationship** — TVG is their risk and product design partner
2. **Claims data feed** — payer sends claims data, TVG enriches with outcomes and risk scores
3. **Contract co-design** — TVG's contract engine designs value-based products the payer can offer
4. **Shared analytics dashboard** — payer and provider both see the same performance data, reducing disputes

### Regulator Integration Model
TVG interfaces with regulators through:
1. **CCHI PROMs reporting** — automated export of PROMs data in CCHI-mandated format
2. **CHI bundle alignment** — TVG's bundle catalog maps directly to CHI's 7 priority conditions
3. **CNHI procurement support** — TVG-formatted outputs meet national procurement requirements
4. **Sandbox participation** — TVG applies for and operates within SAMA / MOH regulatory sandboxes to pilot new payment models

## PART NINE — COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

### The Moat

TVG's competitive moat compounds across three dimensions:

**Data Moat**
Each provider on Layer 1 contributes to a cost and outcome benchmark that no competitor can replicate without the same network. The benchmark becomes more accurate and more defensible as the network grows.

**Regulatory Moat**
MGA licensing, CHI alignment, CCHI PROMs certification, and NDMO compliance create regulatory barriers that take 2–3 years to replicate. First-mover advantage is structurally significant.

**Relationship Moat**
Layer 1 RCM teams are embedded in hospital billing departments. The switching cost of replacing a deeply integrated RCM platform is measured in years of data migration risk and staff retraining. Each contract signed at Layer 1 creates a 5–7 year lock-in window for Layers 2–4.

### Against Global Competitors
Global RCM players (R1, Ensemble, Cotiviti) have Saudi market interest but lack:
- Arabic clinical NLP at the depth required for ICD-10 CM/PCS coding in Arabic
- Knowledge of Saudi payer mix, NPHIES architecture, and CHI-specific requirements
- Regulatory relationships with SAMA, CCHI, and CHI

TVG's local intelligence is the only asset that cannot be acquired through capital alone.

## SUMMARY — THE STRATEGIC LOGIC IN ONE PARAGRAPH

Theeb Value Group enters the market through the most acute pain point in Saudi healthcare — revenue leakage and coding inaccuracy — and deploys Integra RCM to solve it at the provider level. Every solved claim generates structured clinical-financial data that funds and feeds the next layer. Layer 2 uses that data to build a smarter MGA that traditional insurers cannot replicate. Layer 3 aggregates provider and payer data with patient-reported outcomes to create the first Saudi-native healthcare performance intelligence platform. Layer 4 closes the loop — using all of the above to price, execute, and settle outcome-based contracts at scale. By the time competitors recognize the market, TVG will have built data assets, regulatory relationships, and switching costs that make it structurally unassailable as the healthcare financial infrastructure of the Arabian Gulf.
