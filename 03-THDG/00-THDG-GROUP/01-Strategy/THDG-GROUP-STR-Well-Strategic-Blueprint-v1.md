## The Problem We Solve

Saudi Arabia spends SAR 196B ($52.2B) annually on healthcare, yet the primary care experience is fundamentally fragmented:
- Long waits, no continuity of care
- Isolated telehealth apps with random doctors
- Escalating specialist visits for manageable conditions
- Insurer loss ratios hitting 83–96% due to unmanaged chronic claims
- Obesity and diabetes costing the economy $19.38B annually

**No competitor operates a full hybrid (physical + digital) integrated care model. That is THDG's strategic position.**

## The Well Platform Architecture

Well delivers an integrated healthcare platform orchestrating the entire patient journey through three layers:

| Layer | Component | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Physical | Modern Clinics | Hospitality-grade urgent care, same-day access 8AM–10PM, walk-in FFS with NPHIES insurance integration |
| Digital | 24/7 Virtual Access | 'Treat Me Now' instant access, secure messaging/video, Family Doctor subscription model |
| Intelligence | Proactive Care | B2B employer health dashboards, CDM, wellness tracking |

**Core principle:** A unified fee-for-service model capturing value across every healthcare touchpoint.

## Corporate Architecture

```
WellUnion (HoldCo) — THDG-WEL
├── WellCare (MSO) — Holds MOH licenses, receives hybrid management fee (Base + 2% UC revenue + quality bonus)
├── WellTech (PropCo) — IP, EHR, digital platform; receives 6-8% of revenue as incentive fee
└── WellClinics (AssetCo) — THDG-UCL
    Owns all 71 clinics + all 7 revenue divisions
    Bears all direct costs, captures 100% external patient/insurer revenue
```

**Strategic intent:** Isolates clinical liability, centralizes IP valuation at tech multiples, enables asset-backed debt financing for clinic buildout.

## Competitive Position

Well occupies the unique position of **High Tech + Full Hybrid** in the Saudi primary care market:

- **Telehealth (Cura, Nala):** No physical clinics, no labs — virtual only
- **Pharmacy Clinics (NahdiCare):** Retail convenience, no relationship
- **Hospital Groups (HMG, Mouwasat):** Hospital-centric, incentivize higher acuity

**Competitors are built for episodic sickness. Well is engineered for continuous health.**

## The Margin Staircase (Inter-Divisional Synergy)

Each division added multiplies customer LTV while shared infrastructure costs remain flat:

1. Patient enters for standard Urgent Care consultation → **Base Margin**
2. Labs + pharmacy referrals captured on-site → **Margin Steps Up**
3. Patient initiates virtual Quick Consult using UC doctor's downtime → **Zero Incremental Fixed Cost**
4. Patient orders STD test via Men's Health, delivered by Home Care fleet → **Leverages Existing Logistics**
