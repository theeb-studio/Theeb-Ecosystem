# Union Clinics — Clinic Network Strategy

## Entity Overview
**Name:** Union Clinics (WellClinics AssetCo)
**Role:** Physical clinic network — the hub of the Well integrated care ecosystem
**Model:** Urgent Care walk-in clinics operating 8AM–10PM, hospitality-grade patient experience
**Insurance:** NPHIES/CCHI integrated billing

---

## Strategic Position: The Hub-and-Spoke Model

Union Clinics functions as the **physical anchor** from which all other THDG services are activated:

```
Union Clinic (Hub)
├── In-clinic: Consultation + Labs + Pharmacy (TAP) + Procedures
├── Outbound: Home Care fleet dispatched from clinic base
├── Digital: UC doctors serve Virtual Care / Quick Consult during downtime
├── Specialty: Men's Health tests dispatched via Home Care fleet
├── Continuity: CDM enrolled patients tracked through unified EHR
└── Referral: Mental Health marketplace accessed through clinic app
```

## Revenue Streams Per Clinic

### 1. Walk-in Consultations (Primary)
- Consultation fee + labs + procedures + medications
- Blended revenue per visit: **SAR 152.50**
- Net collection rate: **74.6%** with CCHI insurers
- Daily target at maturity: ~27 visits/day

### 2. B2B Corporate Accounts
- PEPM contracts with employers: **SAR 50–150/month**
- Captive employee population drives guaranteed utilization
- HR analytics dashboards delivered via WellTech

### 3. Home Care Transfer Fees
- Clinic transfers patients to Home Care fleet
- Transfer fee: **SAR 120–300 per dispatch**
- Turns idle capacity into incremental revenue

### 4. In-Clinic TAP Pharmacy
- All prescriptions fulfilled in-clinic (captive)
- Revenue per prescription captured entirely within THDG ecosystem

---

## Staffing Model

- Fully outsourced clinical staffing: **SAR 90,000/month per clinic**
- No permanent physician headcount on WellClinics books
- MSO (WellCare) manages clinical quality and MOH compliance
- Nitaqat targets: 30% Saudi (Y1) → 50% Saudi (Y5)

## Expansion Roadmap

### Year 1 — Riyadh Launch (11 Clinics)
- Prove unit economics in controlled geography
- Secure first B2B corporate contracts
- Target neighborhoods: High-density commercial + residential districts
- Capital required for buildout: **SAR 5.5M (SAR 500K per clinic)**

### Year 3 — Expansion (41 Clinics)
- Enter Jeddah and Eastern Province
- Each new city follows same B2B-first acquisition model
- Clinics 12+ funded from operating cash flow

### Year 5 — National Platform (71 Clinics)
- Full national coverage in 3 major metro areas
- **SAR 479M total group revenue**
- **~41.7% EBITDA margin**

## Site Selection Criteria
1. High footfall commercial/mixed-use areas
2. Proximity to major employers (B2B anchor clients)
3. Accessible parking (Saudi urban context)
4. 300–500 sqm ground floor preferred
5. Proximity to existing TAP pharmacy locations (shared logistics)
